Dark Horizons

Inspired by the film Interstellar and the broader science fiction genre, this project reflects my endeavour to craft space visuals utilising a variety of tools and techniques. The creative process involves generating concept designs in Adobe Illustrator and Cinema4D, followed by post-production work in Adobe Photoshop and After Effects.

Art Direction
Personal Project

Design & Post Production

During the design phase, the exploration focused on shapes and patterns in both 2D and 3D to fashion compositions that strike a balance between a high-tech aesthetic and organic elements - enhancing the overall immersive experience. Adobe Illustrator served as the primary tool for creating 2D concepts, where various patterns and dotted designs were refined and explored.

3D Shapes

Cinema 4D was chosen as the preferred 3D tool to delve into its features - providing the ideal opportunity for exploration. The 3D phase pushed further into volumetric compositions, where light, geometry, and depth were combined to achieve the cinematic feel referenced from science fiction film environments.

Final Renders

The goal for the final renders was to produce design closer to a game or movie poster - something to proudly display on the office wall. Post-production work in Adobe Photoshop and After Effects brought the compositions to life, combining the 2D and 3D elements into cohesive, visually powerful pieces.
