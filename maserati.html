Maserati - Next Gen. Experience

As a huge fan of the brand, I was very honoured to be part of the design team responsible for the creation of the User Experience for the new generation Maserati vehicles. Our task was to craft the UX for the Infotainment screen - aligning with the latest design trends while accommodating a large number of applications. The project spanned 24 months, from concept to production across three vehicle models including the Maserati Grecale.

Due to NDA, I am not able to show all details.

Project Lead - UI/UX, Motion Graphics
Maserati

Adaptability Requirements

One crucial aspect of the UX design was its adaptability to various contexts. This included the implementation of a night mode, ensuring optimal visibility and reduced strain on the user's eyes during low-light conditions. Additionally, the UX had to be seamlessly tailored for Arabic users, accommodating their unique language and reading preferences. This involved meticulous attention to the interface's layout, typography, and alignment to ensure a seamless experience for Arabic-speaking users.

Furthermore, the UX design had to accommodate right-hand drive configurations. This meant intelligently flipping essential menu items and components to match the specific needs and expectations of drivers in regions where vehicles are right-hand driven. This adaptation ensured that the interface remained intuitive and ergonomic, regardless of the vehicle's drive configuration.

Key Features

Some of the key features we were tasked with included critical aspects such as climate conrol, ambient lighting, vehicle light controls. The UX for seat functions was another focal point, enabling users to adjust seating positions and settings effortlessly, enhancing both comfort and ergonomics.

Beyond these essentials, our team tackled the complexity of UX for suspension controls, allowing users to tailor their driving experience based on road conditions. Furthermore, we navigated the intricacies of cabrio controls, ensuring a smooth transition for users who wanted to enjoy an open-roof driving experience.

Prototype

The prototype was used to simulate the final outcome of the product, developed to cover complete functionality - animations, button interaction, and user flow testing. It was also used to engage in discussions and gather feedback from the customer. The Maserati UX system seamlessly found its place in three distinct vehicle models, becoming an integral part of the Maserati brand experience.

In Production

The Maserati UX system seamlessly found its place in three distinct vehicle models, becoming an integral part of the Maserati brand experience. This UX system played a pivotal role in translating the luxurious essence of the brand into a digital realm.

By ensuring a seamless and visually appealing user experience across multiple models, the Maserati UX system contributed significantly to enhancing the overall perception of luxury and class associated with the Maserati brand.

Media

The Maserati models garnered significant media attention, with one of the key focal points being the in-vehicle User Experience (UX). The intuitive and sophisticated UX design in Maserati vehicles became a standout feature, drawing praise and admiration from both automotive enthusiasts and industry experts alike

Conclusion

The project spanned 24 months, from concept development to production initiation. The key challenge was meeting the standards set by the Italian design team while ensuring the application remained intuitive, optimized, and user-friendly. The close collaboration with Maserati's design studio and the iterative prototyping process were instrumental in achieving a result that elevated the brand's digital presence.
