Genesis - AI Home Intelligence Platform

Genesis is a Generation 3 AI-native home intelligence platform designed for premium residential living. As the founding designer and later Product Design Lead, I played a key role in building the entire product experience from scratch - UI screens, interaction and real time animations, 3D avatar flow, and design system. The platform centers around Teya, a human-like AI home steward supported by a team of specialized AI Expert agents covering Sleep, Health, Stress, Physio, Home Utility, Entertainment, and Kids Learning - all working together to make the home proactively care for its residents.

Lead Product Designer
Living Homes / Etisalat

The Design Challenge

Designing for a home is fundamentally different from designing for a car or a device. The home is where people sleep, recover, raise children, and feel most vulnerable. Every interface decision had to feel caring, not clinical - intelligent, not intrusive.

The core challenge was making an extremely complex AI system feel effortless. Seven specialized AI agents, live health and environmental data, a conversational avatar, and real-time home automation - all had to come together into a single coherent experience that a resident would trust from day one. Building this as the founding designer meant there was no existing design language, no component library, no precedent. Everything was created from zero.

Technology

Living Homes is a multi-device platform running simultaneously across three surfaces - TV (via NUC computer), tablet, and mobile - each with its own interaction model, layout logic, and ambient behavior, all in sync.

The platform runs on a Gen-3 AI-native architecture - an LLM reasoning layer, multi-agent system, and Knowledge Graph that builds a living digital twin of the home and its residents. Edge computing keeps all data private and local by default.

To bring the experience to life, I worked across the full production toolchain. Unreal Engine was used for high-fidelity environments and avatar rendering on the TV surface. Blender handled 3D asset creation and scene composition. Rive powered all real-time animations, responding live to data and state changes. Figma and standard UI/UX tools covered the full design system, interaction flows, and multi-surface specs across all three devices.

Key Features

At the heart of the platform is Teya - a hyperrealistic 3D AI avatar and home steward that listens, reasons, and acts, replacing traditional app navigation with natural spoken interaction across all surfaces.

The Sleep Guardian monitors through the night, automatically adjusting the bedroom environment in real time, while the Health AI dashboard tracks readiness, vitals, and behavioral patterns - surfacing proactive alerts before issues arise.

The Utility AI Expert delivers a live room-by-room view of all environmental data across every zone, identifying risks before they become issues. The Physio AI Expert provides real-time movement analysis with form comparison and post-session reports, while the Stress AI Expert transforms the entire room environment automatically in response to detected stress.

The tablet serves as the primary home control unit for custom automations and room controls. The mobile app provides quick access to key health and sleep metrics and essential home utility options on the go.

Business Outcomes

Living Homes established itself as the only Gen-3 AI home platform in the European premium residential market, standing clearly apart from legacy Gen-1 and Gen-2 players. The product design and user experience became a major selling point for investors - directly contributing to $10M in seed funding and $5M in additional partnership contracts.

The platform was deployed to 5,000 homes in Q1 2026, with projections reaching 50,000 by end of 2026. The design enabled a recurring services revenue model built around health, wellbeing, and lifestyle subscriptions - the highest-value layer in the entire smart home stack.

The software-defined architecture ensures the home never becomes obsolete, with continuous feature rollout requiring no hardware replacement. The platform proved compelling enough for luxury residential developer partnerships, offered as a built-in differentiator at property handover.

The strongest validation came externally - Living Homes was acquired by Etisalat, the largest Telecom operator in the Middle East, a direct recognition of the product vision and the experience built around it.

Media & Recognition

Living Homes was an active participant in the world's most prominent technology and innovation stages. The platform was showcased at GITEX Global in Dubai, AI Everything in Dubai and Riyadh, and CES - Consumer Electronics Show, one of the most influential tech events in the world. The platform's presence at these events positioned Living Homes alongside the leading names in AI, smart home, and consumer technology on a global stage.
