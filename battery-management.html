Battery Management System

The project started with an internal company request to create the UX of a platform showing the properties of the battery set in electric vehicles. From iconography and basic battery function display, the project evolved into a complex system displaying Battery status, overall charge and faults, Module statistics, connectivity status and BMC sleep/wake up cycles together with faulty popups and properties. The total project duration was three months from start to final implementation and CES showcase.

Due to non disclosure agreement I can show very limited amount of visual examples, but will be happy to discuss further.

Project Lead - UI, UX
Internal - Visteon

The Challenge

The core challenge was presenting unfamiliar technical data to the average user. Certain menu options contained intricate details such as battery status, module health, and connectivity status. Our task was to devise a user-friendly and clear method to showcase this information - making complex electrical systems immediately readable at a glance.

Design Process

Utilizing the moodboard, the objective was to dive into contemporary trends in automotive UX while creating a style guide that embodied a polished, distinctive appearance while remaining user-centric. In the initial stages, primary emphasis was on conducting user testing right from the inception of ideas. We paid attention to readability and functionality, and experimented with day and night themes and various visual representations to display battery properties.

In the initial stages of concept design, our primary emphasis was on conducting user testing right from the inception of our ideas, even when they were at the diagram and battery cell information stage. We paid attention to the readability and functionality of our designs. A significant part of this exploration involved considering user familiarity. To achieve this, we delved into concepts revolving around day and night themes. Moreover, we experimented with various visual representations to display battery properties.

These visuals were created to resonate with the average user, ensuring that even the most complex technical details were presented in a manner that felt familiar and easily comprehensible.

Final Design

The decision to adopt a brighter theme was driven by the need to enhance readability and ensure optimal visual clarity. The majority of the design followed a consistent 2D card-based layout. To introduce a touch of uniqueness and depth, a 3D element was strategically incorporated into a single menu item - creating a visual anchor that distinguishes the platform from conventional battery monitoring tools.

Conclusion

The reception for the final product was overwhelmingly positive, with numerous commendations received at CES 2023 specifically highlighting the app's UI and usability. This success led to a decision to showcase the same application at both the Geneva Motor Show and CES 2024 - a testament to the quality and impact of the design work achieved within the three-month development timeline.
